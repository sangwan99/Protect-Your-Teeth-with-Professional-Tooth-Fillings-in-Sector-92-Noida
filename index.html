Understanding Tooth Fillings

ATooth fillings are a common dental procedure used to restore teeth damaged by decay. The process involves removing the decayed portion of the tooth and filling the cavity with a material that restores its normal function and shape. Fillings help prevent further decay by sealing off spaces where bacteria can enter.

Composite Resins

Composite resins are a popular choice for tooth fillings due to their natural appearance. They can be closely matched to the color of your existing teeth, making them ideal for visible areas. Composite resins bond directly to the tooth structure, providing additional support and requiring less removal of the natural tooth.

Amalgam Fillings

Amalgam fillings, made from a mixture of metals, are known for their durability and strength. They are typically used for fillings in the back teeth, where the forces from chewing are greatest. While not as aesthetically pleasing as composite resins, amalgam fillings are a cost-effective and long-lasting option.

Porcelain Fillings

Porcelain fillings, also known as inlays or onlays, are custom-made in a dental laboratory and then bonded to the tooth. They are highly durable and stain-resistant, making them an excellent choice for restoring both function and aesthetics. Porcelain fillings are often used for larger cavities or when additional tooth structure needs to be reinforced.
